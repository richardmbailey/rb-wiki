Compiled knowledge is durable synthesis stored in the wiki so future queries can reuse it instead of reconstructing it from scratch.

# Definition

In this wiki, compiled knowledge means source-backed pages that preserve useful answers, distinctions, methods, and decisions as maintained Markdown artifacts.

# Key Claims

- Reusable query answers should become [Synthesis](/syntheses/llm-wiki-operating-model.md) pages when they improve future routing.
- Compilation differs from one-off retrieval because it changes the knowledge base after use.
- [Wiki Drift](/concepts/wiki-drift.md) is the long-term risk when compiled pages stop reflecting newer evidence.
